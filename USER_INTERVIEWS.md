# User Interview 

## Profile
- **Name:** Rakesh Ch
- **Role:** Engineering Manager
- **Company Stage:** Mid-size startup

## Direct Quotes
1. "The design actually looked really polished. I trusted it pretty quickly."

2. "I liked the idea a lot, but the result confused me a bit. It said ‘OPTIMIZED’ and then also said we were overspending."

3. "I went looking for the Pricing Data page because I wanted to understand where the numbers came from. I expected at least some sample data there."

## Most Surprising Insight
> "The polished design made the confusing results stand out more. If the site looked rough, I probably wouldn’t care as much."

## Key Takeaways
- Users expect the audit summary and recommendations to stay logically consistent.
- Contradictory messaging reduces trust in the AI analysis.
- Users want visibility into pricing sources and sample pricing data.
- A polished UI increases expectations for accuracy and clarity.

## Design Changes Based on Feedback
- Align audit status labels with recommendation logic.
- Improve explanation clarity for “optimized” vs “overspending” scenarios.
- Add visible sample pricing data to the Pricing Data page.
- Increase transparency around how audit calculations are generated.
